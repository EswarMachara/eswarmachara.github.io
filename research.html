Research

My research interests span medical image analysis, computer vision, and deep learning. I am particularly interested in developing robust models for cardiac imaging, histopathology, and domain-adaptive visual recognition. Below are my publications and ongoing work.

Publications

DebrisVision: Bridging the Synthetic-to-Real Gap for Enhanced Underwater Debris Analysis

Emnet Tesfaye Retta*, Sai Manikanta Eswar Machara*, et al.

ICCV 2025 International Conference on Computer Vision (CVAUI & AAMVEM Workshop)

Paper   |   Code   |   Project Page

We address the sim-to-real gap in underwater debris detection by combining synthetic data generation with domain adaptation techniques. Our approach leverages photorealistic rendering and adversarial training to achieve state-of-the-art results on real underwater imagery.

CIPS-Net: Cross-Layer Instance-Prototype Similarity Based Feature Aggregation for Multi-Task Perception

Sai Manikanta Eswar Machara, et al.

Under Review

Preprint (Coming Soon)   |   Code (Upon Acceptance)

A novel feature aggregation mechanism for multi-task dense perception that dynamically weights multi-scale features based on instance-prototype similarity. CIPS-Net achieves competitive performance on autonomous driving benchmarks while maintaining parameter efficiency.
